            The COVID-19 pandemic in Nepal is part of the worldwide pandemic of
 coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) caused by severe acute
  respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2).
   The first case in Nepal was confirmed on 23 January 2020 when
    a 31-year-old student, who had returned to Kathmandu from Wuhan
     on 9 January, tested positive for the disease.
      It was also the first recorded case of COVID-19 in South Asia.
      Nepal's first case of local transmission was confirmed
       on 4 April in Kailali District. The first death occurred on 14 May.
        A country-wide lockdown came into effect on 24 March 2020, and ended 
        on 21 July 2020.[5] As of 26 July 2022, 
        the Ministry of Health and Population (MoHP) has confirmed
         a total of 984,475 cases, 968,802 recoveries, and 11,959 deaths in the country.
